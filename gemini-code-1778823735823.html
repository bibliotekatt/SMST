Callista to Ellucian Student (Banner) Core Concepts

1. Foundational Time Concepts

Moving from 600+ teaching periods in Callista to a streamlined structure in Banner[cite: 14, 16].

ADMINISTRATIVE

Term

The broad administrative block (e.g., Semester 1, Trimester 2)[cite: 10, 20]. Controls fees, enrolment, and reporting[cite: 21].

FLEXIBILITY

Part of Term

The specific teaching period within a Term[cite: 11, 51]. Supports intensive delivery or late start variants[cite: 53].

HISTORICAL

Effective Term

Defines when data becomes active[cite: 66]. Preserves historical accuracy by adding new "points in time" instead of overwriting[cite: 71, 72].

2. Record Organization

In Banner, identity and academic status are separated into distinct record types[cite: 134, 138].

General Person → Learner → Curricula → Outcome
  • General Person Record: Shared identity (Name, DOB) for students, staff, and alumni[cite: 151, 152].
  • Learner Record: High-level student status (Residency, overall status)[cite: 154].
  • Curricula Record: Manages the active course of study and progression[cite: 162].
  • Outcome Record: Captures graduation and formal awards[cite: 164].

3. Concept Comparison: Callista vs. Banner

Concept Callista (Legacy) Banner (New)
Curricula Course attempts/details stored together; Majors optional[cite: 15]. Specific object; Mandatory Field of Study (even if "No Major")[cite: 192, 199].
Study Path Status tracked at the course attempt level[cite: 15]. Separate object tracking lifecycle (Enrolled, Intermission, etc.)[cite: 195].
Field of Study Called "Unit Set Attempts"; often added later[cite: 15]. Mandatory; links course context to unit attempts[cite: 199, 201].
