Go8 Uni: Enrolment Condition Process

Student Academic Progress Framework

Objective

This process ensures procedural fairness by allowing students to respond to proposed conditions before they are formally applied. Enrolment conditions are strictly supportive mechanisms designed to help students return to satisfactory academic standing.

The 5 Enrolment Conditions

1. Underload Enrol in a maximum of X credit points.
2. Required Unit Enrol in and pass a specific designated unit.
3. Restricted Unit Must not enrol in a specific restricted unit.
4. Credit Minimum Enrol in and pass at least X credit points.
5. Course Specific Enrol in specific level or coded units (e.g. Level 3000).

Conversion to 5 Streams

The proposed framework categorizes academic risk into five distinct streams:

Stream 1: Early Support
In-semester outreach for failed assessments or high-risk indicators.
Stream 2: Support to Pass
Students failing units but not yet meeting unsatisfactory progress criteria.
Stream 3: Unsatisfactory Progress
Significant failure rates or breach of previous enrolment conditions.
Stream 4: Slow Progression
Students exceeding 75% of course duration without proportional completion.
Stream 5: Disengaged Students
Students receiving exclusively 'Withdrawn' (ON) grades.

Administrative Implementation

Condition SMST System Automation Review Outcome
Underload Prevents enrolment exceeding credit limit. Triggers Stream 2/3 if limit exceeded and a unit is failed.
Required Unit Blocks general enrolment until unit is added. Triggers Stream 2/3 if unit is failed or not enrolled.
Credit Minimum Reminds student of condition during enrolment. Not met if student passes fewer than required credits.
